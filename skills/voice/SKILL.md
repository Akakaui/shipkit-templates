# VOICE SKILL

Last updated: 2026-06-26
Version: 1
Scope: Content Writer, Editorial Agent, Copy Agent,
       Sales Agent, Client Agent, Humanizer

## WHO THE USER IS AS A WRITER

Casual but sharp. Does not write like he is presenting
at a conference. Writes like he is talking to someone
who is smart but busy. Gets to the point. Does not
pad sentences. Does not use filler.

## VOICE RULES

1. Short sentences. Cut anything that does not earn its place.
2. Active voice always. "I built this" not "This was built by me."
3. No jargon unless the audience expects it.
4. Contractions are fine. "Don't" not "do not" in social content.
5. One idea per paragraph. If a paragraph covers two ideas, split it.
6. Start strong. The first line of any piece is the most important.
7. End with a clear next step. Never let content just stop.

## SENTENCE PATTERNS THAT WORK

- "Here's what happened."
- "This is the move."
- "Most people get this wrong."
- "The fix is simple."
- "I tested this. It worked."

## SENTENCE PATTERNS TO AVOID

- "In today's fast-paced digital landscape..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "Leveraging synergies..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- Any sentence that sounds like a LinkedIn generic post

## TONE SLIDERS

Content type → tone adjustment:

  Social post:    Casual + direct + opinionated
  Thread:         Structured + punchy +每个linestands alone
  LinkedIn:       Professional but human, no corporate speak
  Sales copy:     Confident + specific + benefit-focused
  Client email:   Warm + clear + action-oriented
  Editorial:      Thoughtful + backed by examples + personal take

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC NOTES

LinkedIn:
  - Long-form posts perform well
  - Carousel posts alternate with text posts
  - No hashtags in the body, max 3 at end
  - First 2 lines must hook — this is the "see more" gate

X/Twitter:
  - Threads: each tweet must standalone
  - Hot takes: state opinion first, explain second
  - No threads under 5 tweets — either commit or post single
  - Banter and personality perform better than generic advice

Instagram:
  - Carousels: slide 1 is the hook, slide 2-3 is context, last is CTA
  - Captions: short, conversational, emoji-sprinkled but not overdone
  - Stories: behind-the-scenes, polls, raw moments

Facebook:
  - Visual-first always
  - Longer form than X but shorter than LinkedIn
  - Group posting: add personal context, not just link drops
